## 2. Core Messaging Framework

| Element | Current | New |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Title** | Tech Lead for Business Modernization | AI Productivity Engineer |
| **Tagline** | "I modernize business operations with Google Cloud" | "I modernize business operations with AI to make companies move faster" |
| **Core expertise** | Systems integration (ERP, CRM, custom software) with focus on Google Cloud Technologies | AI-powered automation for repetitive business processes — email, documents, workflows, data entry |
| **Cloud positioning** | GCP is the lead | Cloud is implementation detail (GCP, Azure, AWS — whatever the company has) |
| **Target audience** | Business owners and CTOs at 20-100 person companies using Google Workspace | Business owners, COOs, and ops leaders at companies where manual processes slow them down |
| **MailPilot framing** | "See what I can build on GCP" | "See how AI handles real business communication" |

### Key rules

1. "Google Cloud" disappears from all headlines, hero, cards, CTAs — replaced with "AI"
2. Business problems lead, not technology: "stop manual data entry" not "Cloud Functions and Pub/Sub"
3. "Company" not "team" — the value is business-level speed, not developer productivity
4. Cloud platforms (GCP, Azure, AWS) appear only in: architecture/demo technical details, about page skills, resume

## 5. Ideal Customer Profile updates

### Positioning table

- **Who I am**: Konstantin Borovik — AI Productivity Engineer with 20+ years in systems integration and cloud architecture
- **What I do**: I modernize business operations with AI to make companies move faster
- **Core expertise**: AI-powered automation for repetitive business processes — email, documents, workflows, data entry
- **Target audience**: Business owners, COOs, and ops leaders at companies where manual processes slow them down
- **Proof of work**: lab5.ca — live demo showing AI handling real business email end-to-end

### What I solve (reframed)

1. **Repetitive manual work** — Staff spend hours on email, data entry, document processing, and reporting that AI can handle
2. **Slow business processes** — Orders, approvals, and customer responses take too long because humans are the bottleneck
3. **Disconnected systems** — ERP doesn't talk to CRM, email doesn't connect to documents — data gets copy-pasted between tools
4. **No AI strategy** — Company sees competitors using AI but doesn't know where to start or who can actually build it
5. **Growing pains** — Processes that worked at 20 people break at 100 — automation is the only way to scale without proportional headcount

### Target companies

- **Drop** Google Workspace as must-have
- **Drop** 20-100 employee size restriction — any company size
- **Keep** North America geography
- **Keep** real revenue (not pre-revenue startups)
- **Add** must-have: identifiable manual/repetitive processes that are a bottleneck
- **Decision authority**: Owner/CEO/COO can approve (business decision, not engineering decision)

### Decision makers

- **Primary (co-equal)**: Business Owner/CEO AND COO/Operations Director
- **Secondary**: CTO (technical validation, not the business decision)
- **Drop**: CTO as co-primary

### Industries

Keep the same list — all are strong AI productivity fits. They're paper-heavy, operations-driven industries with massive repetitive work.

### Outreach messaging

- **To business owners**: Lead with "AI makes your company move faster" + demo CTA
- **To COOs**: Lead with specific process automation (email response time, document processing, order handling)
- **To CTOs**: Lead with technical credibility (architecture page, production AI systems)

### Disqualification criteria updates

- **Remove**: "Microsoft-only stack" — cloud platform doesn't matter anymore
- **Keep**: Enterprise procurement too slow, pre-revenue, already has strong technical team, staff aug only
